Project Overview

This study particularly focuses on Denmark and the policy it has adopted for unemployed people. The Danish labor market has implemented a strategy called flexicurity, which addresses people’s rights and duties. This policy outlines the rights of unemployed individuals to receive a basic income and their duty to search for jobs and participate in labor market programs. The paper presents the performance and policy rankings on a scale of 1 to 10 for Denmark and the United States, displayed in a tabular format. Denmark ranks first with a high percentage of happy people, a strong economic background, and excellent insurance benefits. The study shows the history and development of the flexicurity policy and if it is possible for the United States to adopt it (Claus Thustrup Kreiner and Michael Svarer, 2022).

The Danish labor market has a tradition of negotiations between worker class people and employers, primarily concerning wages and the number of working hours of the employees. The government intervenes only when necessary, such as when the parties fail to reach an agreement. In Denmark, unions play a major role, not only participating in bargaining but also engaging in the political processes of the labor market system. In Denmark, there is a policy of flexible hiring and firing combined with high income security, which boosts the overall economic level of the country. Compared to the United States, Denmark provides higher income security and better benefits for unemployed people. The funding for these benefits comes from government subsidies and employer contributions. Even if the initial support is exhausted, Denmark has a backup system to provide additional compensation for unemployed individuals, unlike the United States. And still, the Danish employment rate is higher than the United States. Denmark also provides training and job search assistance for unemployed people in addition to unemployment benefits. Through the Active Labor Market Programs, each individual is matched with a caseworker who works to improve their job prospects and chances of finding employment. The intensity and duration of the program increase with the duration of unemployment. The purpose of this program is to prevent employees from falling below the productivity threshold. In Denmark, the Danish population are almsot same in terms of their educational background, homogenity and more discplined and does work in a uniform way. These workers have high job security and have proper income . Even if they are fired, they have some amount of money provided to them for basic needs and facilities and that is taken from their income when they were earning. The system is very perfect in their own country but will suffer in other foriegn countries. In Countries like the United States, it will be a bigger challenge to follow this flexicurity method because the country has diverse people following different culture, from different background with different skillset and trying to integrate everything into the job market will be complex. The money spent on finding a job for unemployed people in Denamark is way more than the money spent by the U.S. although the United States is much more bigger country and the economy is way high than Denmark. The percentage of money that the United States spent on job benefits for unemployed is 2% which is same as Denmark but the economy varies a lot. (Claus Thustrup Kreiner and Michael Svarer,2022).

The older flexicurity model failed to maintain low unemployment rates despite high income security and longer insurance benefits. The jobs and training provided to unemployed people are not very effective, as they are of low intensity and only offered to those who voluntarily participate. The impact of the collective bargaining system is that firms do not retain people whose productivity is lower than their wages. When young people turn 18, their wages increase by 40%. However, records show that the employment rate of young people decreases, indicating that some young workers lose their jobs when they turn 18. This implies that firms in Denmark do not keep overpaid employees. The positive aspect is that people with low productivity are not exploited and still receive decent wages. Investing more funds in active labor market programs is important to improve people’s skill sets and therefore increasing the employment rate (Claus Thustrup Kreiner and Michael Svarer, 2022).The Danish policy integrates two fundamental concepts: the first involves the flexibility of recruiting and firing employees according to the evolving needs of the organization, while the second focuses on offering substantial benefits to employees who are terminated. Specifically, it ensures that those who lose their jobs receive a portion of their previous income, which helps to provide financial support and stability during their period of unemployment.

library(ggplot2)

# Example data (replace with your actual data)
Month <- -24:24  # Example months from -24 to 24

# Remove rows with missing or non-finite values
df <- na.omit(df)

# Create the plot
ggplot(df, aes(x = Month, y = EmploymentRate)) +
  geom_point(size = 2) +                               # Scatter plot
  geom_smooth(method = "loess", se = FALSE, color = "black") +  # Smooth line
  labs(title = "Employment Rate by Month Relative to 18th Birthday",
       x = "Month relative to 18th birthday",
       y = "Employment rate (percent)") +
  theme_minimal() +
  theme(axis.text.x = element_text(angle = 45, hjust = 1)) +  # Rotate x-axis labels
  geom_vline(xintercept = seq(-24, 24, by = 12), linetype = "solid", color = "black") +  # Add vertical lines

library(ggplot2)
library(reshape2)

# Sample data for the plot
weeks <- 0:50
treatment <- exp(-0.1 * weeks) * 10  # Example data for treatment group
  control <- exp(-0.05 * weeks) * 10  # Example data for control group

# Create a data frame
data <- data.frame(weeks, treatment, control)

# Melt the data for ggplot2
data_melted <- melt(data, id.vars = 'weeks', variable.name = 'Group', value.name = 'Percent')

# Ensure the Group column has the correct factor levels
data_melted$Group <- factor(data_melted$Group, levels = c('treatment', 'control'))

# Create the plot
ggplot(data_melted, aes(x = weeks, y = Percent, color = Group, linetype = Group)) +
  geom_line(size = 1) +
  scale_color_manual(values = c('treatment' = 'blue', 'control' = 'red')) +
  scale_linetype_manual(values = c('treatment' = 'solid', 'control' = 'dashed')) +
  labs(title = 'Treatment vs Control over Time', x = 'Duration in weeks', y = 'Percent') +
  theme_minimal() +
  theme(legend.title = element_blank())

Creating a pull request

GitHub is a popular web-based platform that is used to share our work with others and collaborate on projects. This platform is mostly used in software development projects, allowing team members to view progress and work accordingly, to work on testing and deployment process. It lets creation of repository where multiple files can be stored and also shows the history of the details of the changes and the user who made the change. Creating branches is the special feature of github. Users usually create a branch which is actually a copy of the original code base or package where any changes or experiment can be made and tested. It is widely used for teamwork and hosting websites. I was first introduced to this platform for sharing my work with my colleagues and my professor. The procedure that was followed to host my work on GitHub is mentioned below:

  1. A profile was created on GitHub.
  2. A repository was created.
  3. Then the repository was uploaded using git.The commands to upload the repository is git init, git add, git commit.
    Git is a flexible distributive version control system used by big companies like Google, Netflix,Linkedin, Microsoft and Facebook since it can handle large projects with ease.
  4. A README file was created first.
  5. Then, the files that were ready to be uploaded were committed using the commit button.
  6. It automatically asks for commit comments. We can provide specific comments to let other viewers know about the details of our work.
  7. Finally, the changes are pushed to the repository and completed the process by creating a pull request.

Git commands are run using a Unix-based application, which simplifies the work for developers working on their projects who use the Windows operating system. Git and GitHub are increasingly utilized for project management purposes for data manipulation. In software development projects, a pull request is created to have our work reviewed and discussed for potential changes. Once a pull request is merged into the main branch, it becomes part of the project’s history. It is always better to thoroughly check the work before creating a pull request, as once it is created and merged, reverting it requires committing new changes and creating another pull request to undo or correct the previous ones.
